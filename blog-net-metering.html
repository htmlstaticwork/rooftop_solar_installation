Understanding Net Metering: How to Sell Back Energy

One of the most powerful financial arguments for switching to solar energy is the concept of net metering. Net metering allows residential and commercial solar owners who generate their own electricity to feed information back to the grid and receive credit on their utility bills. Here is how it works and how you can maximize your ROI.

1. Feeding Information to the Grid

During the middle of a sunny day, your solar panels will likely produce more energy than your home actually consumes. With net metering, this excess electricity is exported back to the grid. In many states and countries, your energy meter actually runs backward as you contribute surplus clean power.

2. Earning Bill Credits

The export of energy generates bill credits. These credits can then be used at night or on cloudy days when your own system isn't producing enough power. Most utility companies allow you to carry these credits over month-to-month, helping you offset higher electricity usage during summer or winter peaks.

3. Full vs. Partial Compensation

Not all net metering policies are created equal. Some utilities offer a 1-to-1 credit (meaning every kWh you give is worth the same as a kWh you buy), while others use a "Net Billing" model with lower export rates. Understanding your local utility's policy is crucial for calculating your long-term savings.
