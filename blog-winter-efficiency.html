How to Maximize Your Solar Efficiency During Winter

One of the most common myths about solar energy is that panels stop working during the winter. While it's true that the days are shorter and the sun sits lower in the sky, solar panels are actually more efficient in cold temperatures. However, snow and shorter daylight hours can present challenges. Here is how you can keep your energy production high all winter long.

1. Keep Panels Free of Snow

While a light dusting of snow will usually melt away on its own due to the heat generated by the cells, a heavy accumulation can block sunlight entirely. Use a soft-bristled roof rake to gently remove snow if it's safe to do so. In many cases, the tilt of the panels allows snow to slide off as soon as the sun comes out.

2. Adjust the Tilt Angle

The sun sits lower in the sky during the winter months. If your mounting system allows for it, adjusting the tilt angle of your panels more vertically (closer to 45-60 degrees) will help capture more direct sunlight and allow snow to slide off more easily.

3. Monitor Performance Regularly

Use your SolarWave monitoring app to keep an eye on your daily production. Sudden drops might indicate a specific panel is covered in snow or obscured by winter debris. Early detection ensures you don't lose days of valuable generation.
